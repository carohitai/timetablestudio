Bloom's Taxonomy (Revised — Anderson & Krathwohl, 2001)

Bloom's classifies learning into six cognitive levels — from Remember at the base to Create at the top. Use it to write learning objectives, frame questions, and design assessments that move students from low-order to higher-order thinking. Click each level to see verbs, question stems, and TWS examples.

How to use Bloom's in your TWS lesson plan

  1. For each chapter, target at least one HOTS objective (Analyze / Evaluate / Create) — CISCE specimen papers reward this.
  2. Spread the question types in your worksheet across levels: don't ask only "Define / List".
  3. In the Objective Generator tab, build SWBAT statements using the level-appropriate verbs.
  4. For Pre-Primary / Primary, lean on L1–L3 with concrete activities; for Middle / Senior, push L4–L6.

NEP × CISCE Alignment Quick Notes

  • FLN (Foundational Literacy & Numeracy): Mandatory focus for Cl 1–3 — embed daily.
  • Art & Sport-integrated learning: Use at least one integrated activity per unit.
  • Toy-based & Story-based pedagogy: Pre-Primary to Cl 5 — anchor concepts in narrative or play.
  • Holistic Progress Card (HPC): Reflect cognitive + socio-emotional + creative observations.
  • Multilingualism: R3L (Reading the Three Languages) — Hindi / Marathi / English crosswalk where natural.
  • Competency-Based Assessment: Move from rote-recall papers to scenario-based questions (CISCE has already shifted).

Quick Reference — Cheat Sheet for the Staff Room

Pin this. Print this. Use it during planning periods.

✓ Do — Lesson Plan Essentials

  • Open with a hook in the first 90 seconds (story, image, question).
  • Write 2–4 SWBAT objectives mapped to Bloom's levels — at least one HOTS.
  • Plan for "I do → We do → You do" shifts of responsibility.
  • Build in a 5-minute Evaluate / exit ticket — no lesson without assessment.
  • Tag at least one NEP pillar (Art / Sport / Story / Experiential).
  • Prepare a differentiation plan for 1 struggling + 1 advanced learner.

✗ Don't — Common Pitfalls

  • Don't write objectives starting with "Teach" or "Discuss" — those are activities, not learning outcomes.
  • Don't ask only Remember/Understand questions in your worksheet.
  • Don't end the period without an exit-ticket, oral check, or observation.
  • Don't use 5E for every lesson — it suits inquiry-driven topics best.
  • Don't tick NEP pillars that the lesson doesn't actually demonstrate.
  • Don't ignore the Holistic Progress Card — capture observations weekly.

💡 Bloom's Verb Picks (most-used at TWS)

  • Remember: list, label, name, recall, identify, define
  • Understand: explain, summarise, classify, infer, compare
  • Apply: demonstrate, solve, use, illustrate, calculate
  • Analyze: differentiate, examine, organise, attribute, deconstruct
  • Evaluate: judge, critique, justify, defend, prioritise
  • Create: design, compose, construct, plan, devise, invent

💡 Assessment Variety (mix at least 3 per unit)

  • Diagnostic: KWL chart, pre-test, concept map
  • Formative: exit ticket, thumbs up/down, mini-whiteboard, peer check
  • Performance: demo, presentation, lab, role-play
  • Project: model, poster, journal, podcast, mini-research
  • Summative: chapter test, term exam, portfolio
  • Self/Peer: rubric self-grade, gallery walk feedback

✓ NEP-CISCE Quick Wins per Stage

  • Foundational (Pre-Pri – Cl 2): Toy-based, story-based, free-play, ECCE blocks, FLN every period.
  • Preparatory (Cl 3–5): Art-integrated, simple experiments, vocabulary-rich talk, 3-language exposure.
  • Middle (Cl 6–8): Inquiry-Based, projects, coding, vocational exposure (10-day bagless).
  • Secondary (Cl 9–10): Competency-based MCQs, Socratic seminars, board-style HOTS.

⚠️ ICSE / CISCE Specific Reminders

  • Specimen papers (2025+) carry 30–40% scenario-based questions — train students on stems like "explain why", "justify with reason".
  • Internal Assessment is now 20% in many subjects — design rubrics in advance.
  • Maintain a Resource Folder per chapter: lesson plan + worksheet + assessment + reflection.
  • Honour the CISCE-prescribed text + at least one supplementary reference per chapter.

Recommended Weekly Planning Rhythm at TWS

  1. Mon morning: Plan all 5 lessons of the week using this dashboard (15 min × 5).
  2. Daily after class: Spend 3 min in the "Reflection" box of your printed lesson plan — what worked, what didn't.
  3. Fri afternoon: Review the week's exit tickets to spot 2 students needing intervention.
  4. Sat coordination: Bring 1 printed lesson plan to the subject-coordinator meeting for peer review.
